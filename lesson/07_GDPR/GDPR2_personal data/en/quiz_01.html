True or false?

You always need the consent of a person to process their data.



In case you have another lawful basis for processing the data, you are allowed to do so. For example, if you need the data to fulfil a contract such as sending a package to the address of the client. However, you must ensure that the data is not stored longer than required by law.

You always have to inform individuals if there has been a data leak that concerns their data.



You only have to inform individuals about the incident in case it presents a high risk for them (such as leakage of passwords or if the data was not encrypted). In most cases you have to inform the supervisory authority.
